The Ancient Heart of Morocco

Fez el-Bali, one of the world's largest car-free urban zones, stands as a living testament to medieval Islamic architecture and culture.

Founded in the 9th century, Fez has been Morocco's spiritual and intellectual center for over a millennium. Its medina is a UNESCO World Heritage Site where narrow alleyways lead to hidden palaces, ancient madrasas, and the world's oldest continuously operating university.

The city's famous tanneries have been dyeing leather using traditional methods for centuries, while artisans continue to create exquisite ceramics, textiles, and metalwork in workshops that have remained unchanged for generations.

Quick Facts

Cultural Highlights of Fez

Fez el-Bali Medina

The world's largest car-free urban zone, a labyrinthine medieval city with over 9,000 streets and alleys dating back to the 9th century.

Chouara Tannery

The famous leather tannery with colorful vats where traditional leather dyeing methods have been preserved for over 1,000 years.

Al-Qarawiyyin University

Founded in 859 AD, recognized by UNESCO as the world's oldest continuously operating degree-granting university.

Top Things to Do

Artisan Workshops

Visit traditional craftsmen creating ceramics, carpets, and metalwork using techniques passed down through generations.

Nejjarine Fountain

Admire the beautifully carved 18th-century fountain and visit the nearby Museum of Wooden Arts & Crafts.

Culinary Tours

Taste authentic Moroccan pastilla, slow-cooked tagines, and sip traditional mint tea in historic riads.

Souk Shopping

Navigate the bustling souks for handwoven rugs, leather goods, spices, and traditional Moroccan lanterns.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

March-May and September-November offer ideal weather (18-25Â°C). Summer can be very hot (35Â°C+), while winter nights can be cold. Spring brings beautiful weather for exploring.

Navigating the Medina

Hire a local guide for your first visit. Download offline maps and carry a power bank. Getting "lost" is part of the experience - most paths lead to main squares.

Where to Stay

Traditional riads offer authentic experiences within the medina. For modern amenities, stay in Ville Nouvelle. Book medina accommodations with clear directions.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress conservatively, especially in religious areas. Learn basic Arabic greetings. Bargaining is expected in souks. Always ask before photographing people.
