The World's Largest Salt Flat

Stretching over 10,000 square kilometers, Salar de Uyuni is a surreal landscape that transforms between seasons, creating one of Earth's most spectacular natural phenomena.

During the dry season, the salt flat becomes a pristine white desert with perfect hexagonal patterns, while the rainy season transforms it into the world's largest mirror, reflecting the sky so perfectly that the horizon disappears entirely.

This otherworldly landscape sits at 3,656 meters above sea level and contains over 10 billion tons of salt, along with significant reserves of lithium that power modern technology around the globe.

Natural Wonders of Salar de Uyuni

Mirror of the Sky

During rainy season, a thin layer of water creates perfect reflections, making the salt flat appear like an endless mirror stretching to the horizon.

Hexagonal Salt Patterns

The dry season reveals intricate hexagonal salt polygons formed by evaporation and crystallization, creating a natural geometric masterpiece.

Incahuasi Island

A rocky outcrop in the salt flat covered with ancient giant cacti, some over 1,200 years old, offering panoramic views of the endless white expanse.

Unforgettable Experiences

Photography Tours

Capture perspective-bending photos and stunning sunrise/sunset reflections in this photographer's paradise.

Stargazing

Experience some of the world's clearest night skies at high altitude with minimal light pollution.

Flamingo Watching

Visit nearby colored lagoons to see thousands of pink flamingos feeding in mineral-rich waters.

Multi-day Tours

Explore the Eduardo Avaroa Reserve with geysers, hot springs, and otherworldly rock formations.

Essential Travel Tips

Seasonal Planning

Dry season (May-October): Clear hexagonal patterns, cooler temperatures, better for driving. Wet season (December-April): Mirror effect, warmer weather, limited access to some areas.

Altitude Preparation

At 3,656m elevation, acclimatize in La Paz first. Stay hydrated, avoid alcohol, and consider altitude sickness medication. Pack warm clothes for cold nights.

Photography Essentials

Bring extra batteries (cold drains them fast), lens cloths for salt spray, polarizing filter, and waterproof gear. The bright white surface can damage cameras.

Health & Safety

Wear high SPF sunscreen and sunglasses (snow blindness risk). Pack first aid kit, water purification tablets, and inform someone of your itinerary. Join reputable tours.
