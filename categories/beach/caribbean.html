About Caribbean

The Caribbean is a dazzling region comprising over 700 islands, islets, reefs, and cays scattered across the turquoise waters of the Atlantic Ocean. It’s celebrated for its white-sand beaches, vibrant coral reefs, warm tropical climate, and a laid-back rhythm that instantly puts travelers at ease.

TThe island’s turquoise waters are protected by a coral reef, making it a paradise for snorkeling and marine adventures. Mount Otemanu, the remains of an extinct volcano, stands tall at the center and adds drama to the skyline.

Beyond the beaches, the Caribbean is a cultural melting pot influenced by African, European, and Indigenous heritage, reflected in its music, cuisine, and traditions. From the reggae beats of Jamaica to the luxurious overwater bungalows of St. Lucia, from the colonial charm of Puerto Rico to the carnival spirit of Trinidad and Tobago, every island offers a unique experience. Whether you're snorkeling with sea turtles in the Bahamas, hiking lush rainforests in Dominica, or savoring spicy jerk chicken in a beach shack, the Caribbean delivers both adventure and relaxation in equal measure.

Quick Facts
  • Location: Atlantic Ocean, southeast of the Gulf of Mexico
  • Best Time to Visit: December to April
  • Famous For: Beaches, island hopping, Caribbean music, tropical cuisine
  • Must-See: Jamaica, Bahamas, St. Lucia, Barbados, Dominican Republic
  • Local Dish: Jerk Chicken, Conch Fritters, Callaloo, Roti

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

December to April offers sunny skies and less humidity, perfect for beach lovers

Getting Around

Use ferries or local flights between islands; taxis and rental cars are ideal for exploring locally.

Where to Stay

Resorts in Aruba, villas in St. Lucia, or all-inclusives in the Bahamas, the Caribbean offers a range of luxurious and budget-friendly options.

Dining Tips

Don’t miss local favorites like jerk chicken, seafood platters, and tropical fruits. Many beach shacks offer authentic flavors with stunning views.
