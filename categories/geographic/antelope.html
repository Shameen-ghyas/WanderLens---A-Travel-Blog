Nature's Cathedral of Light

Antelope Canyon is one of the world's most photographed slot canyons, where ethereal light beams dance through narrow sandstone passages carved by millennia of flash floods.

Quick Facts
  • Location: Page, Arizona, USA

Wonders of Antelope Canyon

Mystical Light Beams

During midday hours, ethereal shafts of sunlight penetrate the narrow canyon opening, creating dramatic beams that illuminate the sculpted sandstone walls.

Flowing Sandstone Walls

Millions of years of flash flood erosion have carved smooth, wave-like formations in the Navajo sandstone, creating an otherworldly underground landscape.

Upper vs Lower Canyon

Upper Canyon offers easier access and famous light beams, while Lower Canyon features more adventurous ladders and unique "corkscrew" formations.

Canyon Through the Lens

Visitor Guidelines

Booking & Reservations

Book tours well in advance, especially for peak season (March-October). Photography tours require special permits and cost more but allow tripods and longer stays.

Best Light Conditions

Light beams are most visible from late morning to early afternoon (11am-2pm) during summer months. Winter has fewer but still spectacular light shows.

What to Wear

Wear closed-toe shoes with good grip. Avoid loose clothing or hats that could fall. Bring layers as canyon temperatures differ from surface.

Safety Considerations

Tours may be cancelled due to flash flood risk. Follow all guide instructions. Bring water and be prepared for sand particles. Respect this sacred Navajo site.
