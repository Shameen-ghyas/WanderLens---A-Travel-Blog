About Santorini

Santorini is one of the most spectacular islands in the world, famous for its dramatic views, stunning sunsets, white-washed buildings, and beautiful beaches.

This volcanic island in the Cyclades group of Greek islands is actually what remains after one of the largest volcanic eruptions in history. The eruption created the current caldera, a large crater now filled with sea water, with towering cliffs on three sides.

The two main towns, Fira and Oia, cling to the top of the cliffs and are known for their blue-domed churches, cave houses, and breathtaking views of the volcano and the Aegean Sea.

Quick Facts
  • Location: Cyclades, Greece
  • Best Time to Visit: May to October
  • Famous For: Caldera views, sunsets, white architecture
  • Must-See: Oia, Fira, Red Beach, Akrotiri
  • Local Dish: Fava, Tomatokeftedes

Highlights of Santorini

Oia Village

The most picturesque village in Santorini, famous for its blue-domed churches and whitewashed houses

Red Beach

One of Santorini's most famous beaches with striking red volcanic cliffs and crystal-clear waters.

Famous Sunsets

Santorini is renowned for having some of the most beautiful sunsets in the world, especially from Oia.

Top Things to Do

Beach Hopping

Explore unique beaches like Red Beach, White Beach, Perissa, and Kamari with their distinctive volcanic sands.

Caldera Cruise

Take a boat tour around the caldera, visit the volcanic islands, and swim in the hot springs.

Wine Tasting

Santorini produces unique wines from ancient grape varieties grown in volcanic soil.

Ancient Akrotiri

Visit the remarkably preserved Minoan Bronze Age settlement buried by volcanic ash.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

The best months are May, June, September, and early October when the weather is pleasant and crowds are smaller. July and August are peak season with higher prices and temperatures.

Getting Around

Rent an ATV or car to explore the island. Public buses connect main towns but have limited schedules. Taxis are available but expensive.

Where to Stay

Oia for luxury and sunsets, Fira for nightlife and central location, Imerovigli for quiet luxury, or Kamari/Perissa for beachfront hotels.

Dining Tips

Try local specialties like fava (yellow split pea puree), tomatokeftedes (tomato fritters), and fresh seafood. Book sunset dinner reservations in advance.
