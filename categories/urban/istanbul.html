Bridge Between Worlds

Straddling Europe and Asia, Istanbul's minaret-dotted skyline tells stories of Roman emperors, Ottoman sultans, and modern Turkish vibrancy across its seven hills.

From the Grand Bazaar's labyrinthine alleys to the Bosphorus' ferry rides at sunset, this city blends Byzantine grandeur with contemporary energy. Hagia Sophia's dome has watched over empires, while trendy Beyoğlu cafes buzz with creative youth.

Whether sipping çay in a historic hamam or bargaining for spices, Istanbul engages all senses.

Quick Facts
  • Country: Turkey
  • Best Time to Visit: April-May & Sept-Oct
  • Population: 15.5 million
  • Famous For: Hagia Sophia, Bosphorus, bazaars
  • Iconic Landmark: Blue Mosque

Istanbul's Treasures

Hagia Sophia

The 6th-century marvel that's been a church, mosque, and now museum with a soaring dome.

Grand Bazaar

4,000 shops under colorful vaults in the world's oldest covered market (since 1461).

Bosphorus Cruise

Where Europe meets Asia, with waterfront palaces and fortresses along the strait.

Istanbul Experiences

Historic Sites

Topkapi Palace's jewels and the Blue Mosque's 20,000 Iznik tiles.

Food Adventures

Simit (sesame bread), kebabs, and baklava in Karaköy's eateries.

Turkish Bath

Traditional hamam experience at Çemberlitaş or Ayasofya Hürrem Sultan.

Bosphorus Tour

Sunset ferry ride between continents with Ortaköy Mosque views.

Istanbul in Frames

Essential Tips

Cash & Bargaining

Carry small Turkish Lira notes. Polite haggling expected in bazaars.

Mosque Etiquette

Cover shoulders/knees. Women need headscarves at some mosques.

Getting Around

Istanbulkart for trams/metro. Taxis use meters - insist on "taksimetre".

Local Flavors

Try balık-ekmek (fish sandwiches) by the Galata Bridge at sunset.
