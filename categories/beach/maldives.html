About Maldives

The Maldives is a dreamy chain of 1,200 islands known for its turquoise lagoons, white sandy beaches, and luxurious overwater resorts.

Located in the Indian Ocean, the Maldives is the world’s lowest-lying country and a popular destination for honeymooners, divers, and beach lovers. Its coral reefs are teeming with marine life and offer some of the best snorkeling and scuba diving experiences.

The capital city, Malé, is a vibrant hub, but most visitors prefer the serene private resorts scattered across the atolls, where each island often hosts just one resort.

Quick Facts
  • Location: Indian Ocean, South Asia
  • Best Time to Visit: November to April
  • Famous For: Overwater bungalows, coral reefs, luxury resorts
  • Must-See: Baa Atoll, Maafushi Island, Malé, Vaadhoo Island (Glowing Beach)
  • Local Dish: Mas Huni, Garudhiya (fish broth)

Highlights of Maldives

Overwater Bungalows

Iconic luxury villas built on stilts above turquoise lagoons, offering privacy and stunning views.

Glowing Beach (Vaadhoo)

Experience bioluminescent plankton lighting up the shore at night—an otherworldly natural wonder.

Snorkeling & Diving

Swim with manta rays, sea turtles, and colorful reef fish in some of the clearest waters on Earth.

Top Things to Do

Island Hopping

Visit local islands and private resorts, each with unique charm, cuisine, and beaches.

Scuba Diving

Explore shipwrecks, coral reefs, and underwater caves with visibility up to 50 meters.

Luxury Spa Treatments

Relax with traditional Maldivian massages and wellness rituals in serene oceanfront settings.

Sunset Cruises

Enjoy breathtaking sunsets on traditional dhoni boats, often accompanied by dolphins.

Maldives Through the Lens

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Visit between November and April for dry, sunny weather. May to October brings rain but fewer crowds and better rates.

Getting Around

Seaplanes and speedboats connect most islands. Domestic flights also operate between atolls.

Where to Stay

Stay at private resort islands for luxury, or local islands like Maafushi for budget options and cultural experiences.

Dining Tips

Try local flavors like Maldivian curry and coconut-based dishes. Resort dining can be pricey, opt for full board deals.
