About Bora Bora

Bora Bora is a dreamy South Pacific island in French Polynesia, famous for its crystal-clear lagoons, luxurious overwater bungalows, and lush green volcanic peaks. Often called the “Pearl of the Pacific,” it’s a top destination for honeymooners and luxury travelers.

TThe island’s turquoise waters are protected by a coral reef, making it a paradise for snorkeling and marine adventures. Mount Otemanu, the remains of an extinct volcano, stands tall at the center and adds drama to the skyline.

At the heart of the island rises Mount Otemanu, a dramatic remnant of an extinct volcano, which towers over the landscape with rugged green slopes. This peak adds a sense of adventure and wonder, especially when viewed during sunrise or from a helicopter tour. Bora Bora’s main village, Vaitape, offers a peek into local life, with markets, small eateries, and traditional Polynesian culture. Whether you're enjoying a sunset cruise, diving with manta rays, or relaxing on the white-sand Matira Beach, the island delivers a blend of natural beauty and indulgent escape like no other.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Bora Bora is May to October, during the dry season when the skies are clear and humidity is lower. November to April is off-season with occasional rain and better deals.

Getting Around

Bora Bora is small, renting a bike or scooter is the most flexible way to explore. There are also taxis, small buses, and boat transfers between resorts and the main island.

Where to Stay

For overwater luxury, stay at resorts like Four Seasons, St. Regis, or Conrad. On a budget? Choose small guesthouses or inland lodges in Vaitape or Matira Beach.

Dining Tips

Try local dishes like Poisson Cru (raw fish in coconut milk), mahi-mahi, and tropical fruits. Many resorts offer all-inclusive meals, but don't miss out on visiting local cafes for authentic Polynesian flavors.
