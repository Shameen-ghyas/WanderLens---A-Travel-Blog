The Heart of the Inca Empire

Cusco is a vibrant blend of ancient Inca architecture and colonial charm.

Once the capital of the Inca Empire, Cusco is a living museum with cobblestone streets, majestic ruins, and a rich cultural tapestry.

From the awe-inspiring Sacsayhuamán fortress to the bustling San Pedro Market, Cusco invites you to step into history.

Quick Facts
  • Location: Andes Mountains, Peru
  • Best Time to Visit: May–September
  • Must-See: Machu Picchu, Sacsayhuamán, Plaza de Armas
  • Local Dish: Cuy, Lomo Saltado, Chicha Morada
  • Iconic Experience: Exploring Inca ruins

Cultural Highlights of Cusco

Sacsayhuamán

An impressive Inca fortress with massive stone walls, offering panoramic views and a glimpse into ancient engineering.

Plaza de Armas

The historic heart of Cusco, surrounded by colonial architecture and vibrant with local life and culture.

Machu Picchu

The iconic Inca city nestled in the Andes, a UNESCO World Heritage site and a must-visit from Cusco.

Top Things to Do

Machu Picchu

Explore the breathtaking ruins of this ancient Inca city, a marvel of architecture and a bucket-list destination.

Sacsayhuamán

Marvel at the massive stone walls of this Inca fortress, showcasing the precision of ancient Andean engineering.

San Pedro Market

Immerse yourself in local culture at this bustling market, filled with vibrant textiles, fresh produce, and authentic Peruvian food.

Rainbow Mountain

Hike to Vinicunca, the stunningly colorful mountain, for a surreal natural spectacle in the Andes.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Visit during the dry season (May–September) for clear skies and ideal hiking conditions. Avoid the rainy season (December–March).

Getting Around

Walk the compact city center or use affordable taxis. For Machu Picchu, book train tickets in advance.

Where to Stay

Stay near Plaza de Armas for easy access to attractions or in San Blas for a bohemian vibe.

Cultural Etiquettes

Respect local customs, ask permission before photographing people, and acclimate to the high altitude gradually.
